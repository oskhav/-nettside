Vancouver is located on the west coast of Canada, in the province British Columbia. Vancouver has over 660 000 residents in the city and around 2.6 million in the Greater Vancouver. English is the most commonly spoken language. But Vancouver is one of the most ethnically diverse cities in Canada, with nearly 50 % of its population identifying as a visible minority. Vancouvers Chinatown is one of the largest and oldest in North America.


Vancouver is one of the greenest cities in the world, with ambitious sustainability goals. The city aims to be the greenest city in the world by 2025. Vancouver is very bike-friendly. Vancouver is located on the traditional territories of the Musqueam, Squamish and Tsleil Wautuh peoples. These Indigenous communities play an important role in the citys culture and history


Vancouver is often ranked highly for quality of life, but it also grapples with significant social issues. The city have a lot of homeless inhabitants. The downtown eastside is one of the poorest neighborhoods in Canada.


Canada is a federal state consisting of ten provinces and three territories. The political system is parliamentary and democratic.
The British monarch is the country’s formal head of state, represented de facto by a governor general at the federal level and by a lieutenant governor in each of the provinces. The governor general is appointed by the monarch on the advice of the Prime Minister, formally for an indefinite period but usually for a five-year term.
