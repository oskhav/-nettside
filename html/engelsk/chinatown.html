Chinatown

Chinatown in Vancouver is one of the oldest and largest Chinatowns in North America. It was established in the late 19th century by Chinese immigrants who came to work in British Columbia, particularly during the construction of the Canadian Pacific Railway. In Chinatown you can visit classical chinese gardens, museums and traditional buildings. In the entrance of Chinatown you’ll find «The Millennium Gate». It serves as a symbolic gateway to the neighborhood. It’s a popular spot for photos and marks the entrance into this historic district. There is different dinningplaces in Chinatown.
